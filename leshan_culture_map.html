About the Leshan Confucian Temple & Former Wuhan University Site

The Leshan Confucian Temple was first built during the Wude era of the Tang Dynasty and is one of the most well-preserved ancient Confucian temple complexes in southwestern China. During the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression, Wuhan University relocated to Leshan and used the temple as its campus, continuing education here for eight years and cultivating many outstanding talents. It was praised as the "Cambridge of the East."

This project aims to use modern technology to present the historic buildings and heritage relics of the Leshan Confucian Temple and the former Wuhan University site in the form of a digital map, contributing modestly to cultural preservation, academic research, and public education.

Panoramic View of the Leshan Confucian Temple

Most of the existing buildings of the Leshan Confucian Temple were reconstructed during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Covering an area of about 20,000 square meters, it is an important physical resource for studying ancient Chinese architectural art and Confucian culture.

Former Wuhan University Site in Leshan

During the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression, the teachers and students of Wuhan University continued education in Leshan, creating a brilliant legacy praised as "wartime glory, remembered through the ages."
